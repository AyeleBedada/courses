In the following illustration the big black numbers from 1 to 18 are references to the small numbers each with orange circle backgrounds that are 1 to 18 and are annotating the interface of AutoCad. As you mouse hover on the large numbers, you will be able to see some information about the interface component.

Go Back to the remainder of your interface study once you are done exploring here
1: Title tab of Autodesk AutoCAD and your current project
2:Quick access tool bars and their customization
3.Application button (A): Here you can access main/ basic activities such as saving your work , opening drawing utilities, import, export, and option at the lower right coroner of the dropdown list/ panel.
4’and 4: 4’ is a menu bar that has elements of AutoCAD classic interface where menus are used instead of tabs. Can be tuned-on at the dropdown list of the quick access bar (show/hide menus). 4 is where you will find the default tabs of the annotative AutoCAD interface.
5: The ribbon: Every ribbon is accessed by clicking on any of the tabs in 4. Each ribbon is divided in to specialized set of tools/ commands (panels) separated by an engraved line. Hovering a mouse on any of the tools in a panel under every tab provides access to info about the related tool and its function.
6:Tabs of currently opened projects.
7: Here you will have access to your view ports for: (a) changing or controlling your view ports setups such as changing the number of views you can have (1 or many model space views and work parallel in different area of the same project). For example here you can restore or configure view port list (-) and maximize or configure view port list (+). (b) Here you can chose to display your project from left, right, top, bottom or any of the isometric and views. (c) Here you ca change the type of your project viewing (display), such as: wireframe, hidden, etc.
8: This is your general model space that has theoretically unlimited dimension. In the model space is where you will create, modify and view your objects, even while you are in layout space you are basically there to organize the work you did in the model space for plotting. However you can also create objects in the layout space such as: your title blocks, related notes and viewports.
9: This is the User Coordinate System (UCS). Here you can change the location and alignment of your project origin and alignment of your project to different axis both in 2D and 3D.
10: Here you will find tabs for your model space and your layout. While you can have only one model space that you can modify through the tools indicated in point 7 of this interface list, you can add many layout spaces by clicking on the Add (+) sign next to the two default layout tabs. You can change the name and settings of the default and the new layouts you will be creating. Once you have selected a layout by clicking on one of the layout tabs, you can right click on the and access more settings including the page setup manager where you can set different parameters for your plotting such as: scale, paper size, etc.
11. This is the command line. Here you can interact with the software through typing commands in short keys or full words in order to create, modify, extract data from, plot, etc. your projects. While most users apply the use of the command line in combination with browsing to commands that are visually placed in the menus, ribbons and panels mentioned above, almost every user you would know applies the command line while working on their projects.
12: Most services related to online Autodesk are here. Your account on Autodesk, search, online Autodesk community, app store and the like.
13. Minimize, expand or close all projects opened that are indicated on the tabs mentioned on point 6. If you want to close the projects one at a time, you may close them from the interface component indicated on point 6 or point 16.
14. Click (multiple times to cycle around the options available) on the arrow sown on the left side of the interface component to toggle between different display options of the ribbon and tabs mentioned on point 5 and 6. You can optimize the display to just the tabs on point 6, or to the tabs indicated on point 6 and titles of the panels of the ribbons indicated on point 5. You can also display the items on point 6, titles and buttons of the panel of the ribbons. The arrow on the right side of this interface allows you to do the same from a drop down list.
15. Here unlike 13 you can only close, minimize windows of your project one at a time. You can also close tabs of the projects you are working on from 6.
16. This is the view cube where you can have orthogonal (left, right, top and bottom ) view of your project. view of orthogonal with the top view on default setting. You can also orbit the cube with your mouse and have access to any other angle you would like to view. You can also have perspective views of your project in addition to scaled/ technical views such as isometric and orthogonal
17. This is the steering wheel or navigation wheel. You can not find this view displayed in default in AutoCAD 2021. You will need to right click on your model space and select it and it will appear as a floating tool. If you would be working on 3D project, this tool can provide an enhanced navigation around your model.
18. This is one of the most important component of your interface. The control panel. Here you can (a) toggle from model space to paper space (to layout) and in layout to model through view ports, (b) change the grid setting of your model space, (c) orthogonal, (d) polar, (e) snaps, (f) annotation and (g) annotative settings, (h) scale of drawing in a specific view (h) workspace switching, (h) clearing screen, (I) isolate objects and (j) customization of the control panel it self. Functions such as Ortho, polar, snap, etc. are very important for precision while creating and modifying projects, as such you will usually find your self working with this interface component.
