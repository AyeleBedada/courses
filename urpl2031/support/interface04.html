Control Panel AutoCAD

In the following illustration the big black numbers from 1 to 14 are references ( equivalent ) to the small numbers with orange circle backgrounds that are 1 to 14 as well and are annotating the interface of AutoCad control panel. As you mouse hover on the large numbers, you will be able to see some information about the control panel interface components.

Go Back to the remainder of your interface study once you are done exploring here
1, Model and paper: You can access the settings of the control panel for both model space and paper space (layout) of your project by just clicking on the indicated button.
2, Grid: This allows to control their model space’s grid settings (on/off, spacing, OSNAP options, etc.). There are no grid settings for the paper space
3: Restrict your cursor to specific settings : ORTHOMODE(F8):(allows to draw objects in orthogonal projections). Polar Tracking(F10): allows users to align or restrict their cursor to specific set of angles. ISODRAFT: this service allows users to create drawing in an isometric fashion,. This can come in hand when you are making details for architectural details, or sanitary risers diagrams. Object snap Tracking(F11): here you can turn on and off the snap tracking of your AutoCAD setting. OSNAP(F3): here use can set different snap modes such as midpoint, centers, intersections etc. for their drawings.
4, LWDISPLAY: Here you can turn on and off the display of your Line weight setting in your project.
5, Show annotation object at current scale: Mind the fact that the icons look like the scale rulers profile. Here you can on/off annotation objects on current scale in the model space view. While working on your project, you will notice that annotative objects that are assigned with scale will have an icon mentioned above as a gizmo when you interact with them for modification. If thy are assigned with more than one scale the gizmo will be a pair of the ICONS You can change annotation scale of the current view.
6: Turn on and off addition of scale to annotative objects when annotation scale changes: Mind the fact that the icons look like the scale rulers profile. Here you can activate the function of adding scale to annotative objects when ever you change the scale value displayed.
7:Annotation scale of the current view: Mind the fact that the icons look like the scale rulers profile. Displays the current annotation scale of the model space. All annotative objects in the model space will be displayed according to the scale displayed here.
8: Work Space Switching: Here you can switch between different workspace modes: Drafting and annotations; 3Dbasics and 3D modeling that are available by default. The classical interface is no longer available in this area for the recent versions. From here you can also change settings of the exiting workspaces or customize your own and you can save both.
9: Annotation Monitor: Turns on and off the notification for annotation objects’ constraint dissatisfactions, errors or mis-placements. The icon comes in pairs when its on.
10: Isolate Objects: Isolates selected objects in the models space,
11: Trusted DWG format: checks if a drawing is Autodesk's trusted DWG format.
12: Plot publish details report: provides printing and publishing report,
13: Clean screen: Makes the preview of the AutoCAD window clean/ without tabs and menus.
14: The control panel customization: If you press this button, you will see the complete list of tools available in the control panel. The ones you saw before are the ones shown by default. The complete list of this tools and what the control panel looks like after turning on all the panels can be seen below. It is very important that one knows to use this button f they are not well experience with AutoCAD and would not know how to access the functions from elsewhere such as the command line.
The 29 complete list of control panel’s tools: Coordinates , model space, grid, snap mode, infer constraints, dynamic input, Ortho Mode, Polar Tracking, Isometric Drafting, Object Snap Tracking, 2D Object Snap, Line Weight, Transparency, Selection Cycling, 3D Object Snap, Dynamic UCS, Selection Filtering, Gizmo , Annotation Visibility, Auto Scale, Annotation Scale, Workspace Switching, Annotation Monitor, Units, Quick Properties, Lock Ul, Isolate Objects, Graphics Performance and Clean Screen.
